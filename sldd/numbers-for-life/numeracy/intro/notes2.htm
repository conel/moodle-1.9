Life skills:

There are six main sections to help your students with their learning:

  • In DIY your students will learn to keep to a budget, measure for carpet tiles, buy paint and select window blinds.

  • In Bank they will check a bank balance, pay in a cheque and use a bank card to withdraw cash.

  • In Post Office they will post parcels, buy stamps, pay the gas bill, change notes for coins and get a new tax disc for a car.

  • In Shopping Mall they will tell the time, make an appointment at the optician's, visit the travel shop and buy a lottery ticket.

  • In Supermarket they will weigh fruit and veg, select bakery items, collect medicine from the pharmacy, estimate the cost of the shopping and pay the bill.

  • In Garden Centre they will buy slabs for a new patio, choose a bird feeder, select new plant pots and measure up for garden lights.

Each of these sections is closely mapped to the Adult Core Curriculum at Entry Levels 2 and 3. Some activities extend into Level 1. The curriculum reference numbers are listed in the Curriculum Number Index for Entry Level 2 and 3. This is intended for the tutor's use only. The students will find it easier to use the Alphabetical Index which lists the section contents and number skills in everyday words.

The entire content of the Life Skills sections is shown in a Site Map of the area.

Basic maths:

If your students need help with the six sections, there is an extra section called Basics. In Basics they will be able to revise number skills under five headings:

  1. In Whole Numbers they will look at Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication and Division.

  2. In Fractions and Decimals they will look at Understanding Fractions, Fractions in Action, Understanding Decimals and Decimals as Fractions.

  3. In Shapes and Space they will look at 2D shapes and 3D shapes.

  4. In Common Measures they will look at Recording Information and Charts and Pictograms.

  5. In Data Handling they will look at Recording Information and Charts and Pictograms.

The entire content of the Basics section is shown in the Site Map of the area.

User Instructions:

  • Each of the six Life Skills Sections should be worked on from beginning to end, but there is no pre-set order in which the sections should be studied.

  • The Basics section can be used to support the number work in these sections.

  • Links between the Life Skills Sections and Basics are found on the Revision Pages.

  • Clicking on the picture symbol in the top right-hand corner of the screen will take the student directly to a Revision Page linking them to relevant Basics material.

  • The Site Maps may be used to inform you of each section's content, so that you may choose the section that you want the student to study.

  • Alternatively, you may let students choose a section themselves at random.

The student will then:

  • Click on the icon for that section.

  • Read the text on the screen. They may listen to the text by clicking onto

  • Move to the next screen by clicking .

  • Move back a screen by clicking .

  • Complete the exercises by typing their answers into the answer boxes. The program will tell them if their answers are correct or not.

  • They should click    to print their exercises.

  • They should assemble their completed exercises in a folder for you to check on their progress.

Changing text size:

If your students need larger text:

  • Click View on the menu bar.
  • Click Text Size.
  • Click Largest.

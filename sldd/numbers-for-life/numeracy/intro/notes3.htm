Life skills:

There are six main sections for you to investigate:

  • In DIY you will keep to a budget, measure for carpet tiles, buy paint and select window blinds.

  • In Bank you will check your bank balance, pay in a cheque and use a bank card to withdraw cash.

  • In Post Office you will post parcels, buy stamps, pay the gas bill, change notes for coins and get a new tax disc for a car.

  • In Shopping Mall you will tell the time, make an appointment at the optician's, visit the travel shop and buy a lottery ticket.

  • In Supermarket you will weigh fruit and veg, select bakery items, collect medicine from the pharmacy, estimate the cost of the shopping and pay the bill.

  • In Garden Centre you will buy slabs for a new patio, choose a bird feeder, select new plant pots and measure up for garden lights.

Basic maths:

If you need help with any of these tasks there is an extra section called Basics. In Basics you will be able to revise many number skills within five headings:

  1. In Whole Numbers you will look at Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication and Division.

  2. In Fractions and Decimals you will look at Understanding Fractions, Fractions in Action, Understanding Decimals and Decimals as Fractions.

  3. In Shapes and Space you will look at 2D shapes and 3D shapes.

  4. In Common Measures you will look at Metric Measures, Time, Date and Money.

  5. In Data Handling you will look at Recording Information, Charts and Pictograms.

  • Click the picture   in the top corner to take you to revision material.

  • Complete the exercises by typing your answers into the answer boxes.

  • Click    to print your work.

  • For some of the exercises you are given a score as a percentage, for instance 80%.

    If you get all the questions right, you will score 100%.

    If you get half of the questions right you will score 50%.

    If you get none of the questions right, you will score 0%.

    Any score between 60% -100% is good.

    If you get less than 60%, you need to try again.
